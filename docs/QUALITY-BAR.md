# EMBERFALL — THE QUALITY BAR & PROOF PLAN (max depth, confirmed before art)

> The definitive spec of what "maxed-out quality and depth" means - concrete, testable. We build
> + flesh this out on FREE assets, TEST against this bar, and only buy art once the non-art game
> meets it. This is the checklist that defines "good enough to spend on."

================================================================
## THE STRATEGY (de-risk the unknowns for free, buy art last)
================================================================
Art is the KNOWN-solvable pillar (a cohesive bought pack fixes it). So we prove the RISKY pillars
first, on free placeholder art: does it PLAY well, is it DEEP, is the WRITING good, does it FEEL
professional? If yes -> buy the cohesive pack + re-skin. We never spend until the game is proven
good without art.

================================================================
## THE 6 QUALITY PILLARS (each with a concrete, testable bar)
================================================================

### PILLAR 1 — FEEL / GAME-JUICE (does it feel good moment-to-moment?)
MAXED BAR:
- Responsive controls (no input lag; forgiving timing windows).
- Combat juice: hit-pause on impact, knockback, hit-flash, screen-shake (accessibility toggle),
  weapon-swing arc/trail FROM THE HAND, enemy death poof + coin burst.
- Movement juice: footstep SFX by surface, dust particles, smooth camera follow + lookahead.
- Pickup/UI juice: sparkle on pickup, item-get "hold aloft" pose + jingle, menu nav SFX.
- Smooth scene transitions (fade/wipe, not hard cut).
TEST: combat feels weighty + satisfying; moving around feels good; every action has feedback.

### PILLAR 2 — COMBAT DEPTH (is fighting interesting, not button-mash?)
MAXED BAR:
- Player kit: attack + DODGE/ROLL + BLOCK (i-frames on roll, directional block), chargeable/
  special later. Stamina or cooldown gating so it's tactical.
- Enemy variety: each enemy a distinct QUESTION (charger=dodge, shielded=flank/break, ranged=
  close/cover, swarm=crowd-control), telegraphed wind-ups so it's fair.
- Bosses: multi-phase, bigger, each needs a TRICK (not just hit it) + tests the region tool +
  dodge/block. Readable tells.
- Damage model: gear + stats drive numbers; light vs heavy; weak points.
TEST: a fight makes you THINK + react; a boss feels like a puzzle+reflex test; dodge/block matter.

### PILLAR 3 — RPG DEPTH (is there real progression + character building?)
MAXED BAR:
- XP + LEVELS.
- STATS (DnD-style): STR, DEX/Speed, CON, INT, WIS, CHA - each does something real (combat,
  dialogue options, prices, carry, magic).
- SKILL TREE / learnable abilities (combat moves, magic, social, utility).
- GEAR with STATS (atk/def/spd/magic/effects) + DURABILITY + fuel (sword wears/breaks, lantern
  needs oil); CHARACTER SHEET showing totals.
- JOBS (Fable-style) for money + skill multipliers; ECONOMY (shops buy/sell, fair balance,
  property later).
TEST: leveling + choosing stats/skills feels meaningful; gear choices matter; a character sheet
that reads like a real RPG; money has a satisfying loop.

### PILLAR 4 — WORLD & EXPLORATION (is it big + rewarding to explore?)
MAXED BAR:
- SCALE: ~1hr childhood before catastrophe; big Greenhollow + much more area; objectives spread
  out (travel, not "it's right there"); 5 distinct regions.
- DENSITY: secrets everywhere, searchable houses/drawers/breakables, off-path rewards, vistas,
  clue-hunt threads (Pem etc.), every screen pulls curiosity.
- METROIDVANIA: tools open old areas; telegraphed gates; backtrack rewards + fast travel.
- HONEST WORLD: clamped camera (never off-screen), honest edges, no trap rooms, no black
  interiors, gates that match their state, believable continuity between areas.
TEST: you WANT to explore; looking around is always rewarded; the world feels like a place; ~1hr
of real content before the catastrophe.

### PILLAR 5 — STORY, CHARACTERS & HUMOUR (does it grab people?)
MAXED BAR:
- The spine (Ember/oracle lie) drip-fed, recontextualising, building to 3 earned endings.
- Catastrophe REWRITTEN: build-up + dread + clear explanation (what/why/the time-skip) + karma-
  reactive narrative (good/bad childhood changes the return).
- Characters with depth + variety (no early repeats - lots of distinct NPCs), who react to your
  karma/wealth/choices + remember you.
- HUMOUR + TONE dialed up: cheeky, rude, silly, dark, heartfelt - the full range; comedic/rude/
  sad discovered VIGNETTES (Fable-style); learnable EMOTES; characterful writing (Pip's sword
  gag etc.). R18 edge, never explicit, never minors.
- Choices echo (childhood seeds -> late payoffs); the karma web visibly shapes the world.
TEST: you CARE about Greenhollow before it burns; the catastrophe lands + makes sense; you laugh
+ feel; characters feel distinct + reactive; you want to know what happens next.

### PILLAR 6 — PRESENTATION / PROFESSIONAL POLISH (does it look/sound pro?)
MAXED BAR (the parts we CAN do without bought art):
- TEXT: legible font, ALWAYS fits its box (wrap/paginate), never clipped; balanced.
- NO MESSY OVERLAPS: HUD/labels/dialogue/tracker all in clean reserved zones.
- UI/HUD/MAP: crisp, consistent, professional framing; quality minimap + full map (where-you-are,
  discovered, world map) + character sheet + inventory with icons.
- AUDIO: voice-blips per character; good SFX; context music.
- (ART: placeholder-free-now -> bought cohesive pack LATER = the final pro layer.)
TEST: nothing clips/overlaps/janks; UI reads as a real game; with bought art later it's fully pro.

================================================================
## THE BUILD-AND-PROVE SEQUENCE (flesh out, then test)
================================================================
We build toward the bar in this order, testing as we go. Goal: a PROVEN, deep, polished,
funny, ~professional-feeling game on free art -> THEN buy art.

PHASE A - PROFESSIONAL FEEL (Pillar 6 + 1): text-fit, no overlaps, juice, swing-from-hand, UI/map
quality, voice-blips, gate/interior/black-room fixes, quest tracker (all + short/full + filter).
[Proves: it can FEEL professional + good to play.]

PHASE B - COMBAT + RPG DEPTH (Pillars 2 + 3): dodge/roll/block, enemy variety, XP/levels/stats/
skill-tree, gear stats + durability + fuel, character sheet, jobs, shops/economy. Tutorialise
each. [Proves: real DEPTH.]

PHASE C - WORLD + STORY + HUMOUR (Pillars 4 + 5): 5x the childhood (~1hr), bigger map + more
quests (spread out, funnier framing), searchable world + breakables, comedic/rude/sad vignettes,
emotes, catastrophe rewrite (build-up + explanation + karma-reactive), character variety, clue
threads. [Proves: it's BIG, grabbing, funny, story-rich.]

THE PROOF GATE: after A-C, play it. Does it meet the 6-pillar bar (deep, fun, funny, big-feeling,
professional-feeling) on free art? 
- YES -> BUY the cohesive art pack (Time Fantasy ~$30-50) + re-skin -> now fully professional ->
  continue building out the full 5 regions + endings.
- NO -> we know exactly which pillar fell short + fix it before spending.

================================================================
## RE-SKIN SAFETY (why buying art later is safe, not wasteful)
================================================================
- Build ART-AGNOSTIC: all art referenced via a central asset registry / manifest (tile ids,
  sprite keys, sizes) so swapping the art pack = swapping the manifest + assets, NOT rewriting
  game logic. Keep sizes/conventions consistent (e.g. a target tile size) so a bought pack drops
  in. This makes the later re-skin a content swap, not a rebuild.
- So building on free art now is NOT throwaway: the game logic, systems, levels (by tile id),
  story, and feel all carry over; only the pixels change.

## THE ONE LINE
"Maxed quality" = the 6 pillars at their bar (feel, combat depth, RPG depth, world/exploration,
story/humour, presentation). We build A->B->C on free art to PROVE pillars 1-5 + the doable parts
of 6, test against this bar, and only buy cohesive art once it's proven good - with an art-agnostic
asset layer so the re-skin is a swap, not a rebuild.
