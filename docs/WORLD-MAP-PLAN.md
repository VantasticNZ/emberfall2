## 0. THE STRUCTURE (the key feasibility decision)
**Overworld + separate enterable scenes — NOT one seamless giant map.** The **overworld** (a streamed
chunk world) holds the region **wilds + settlement exteriors + connectors + open-world roaming**. Every
**city, dungeon, and interior is a SEPARATE scene** (the proven Phase-0 `interiorRegion`/`_enterArea`
system) reached by a **door on the overworld**. This is why a 120-tile city doesn't bloat the
overworld — the overworld just carries its *entrance*. The streaming engine already does this (20×20
today; 24×24 is the same proximity-streaming, ~25 chunks live at once). ✅ feasible.

## 6. DERIVED TOTAL DIMENSIONS
- **OVERWORLD: 24×24 chunks = 768×768 tiles (24,576 px²).** (Up from today's 20×20=640; justified by the
  sized+positioned settlements + connectors + roaming space. GH centred at chunk 12,12.)
- **SEPARATE enterable scenes: ~55-70.** = **3 cities** (each several district sub-maps, ~6-10 each) +
  **~10 dungeons** (×2-4 floors ≈ 25 floor-maps) + **~30-50 interiors** (shops/homes/halls/temples).
- **Total authored area** ≈ the 768² overworld **+** ~25 dungeon floors (~30-40 tiles) **+** ~50
  interiors (~12-20 tiles) **+** 3 cities (~100 tiles each, multi-map). The bulk of the "30h" lives in
  the **separate scenes**, not the overworld — which is why 24×24 suffices.
- **Engine feasibility:** ✅ proximity-streaming (~25 chunks live) is unchanged at 24×24; the separate
  scenes load on enter (Phase-0, proven). No new engine work for the *size*; the work is content + art.
