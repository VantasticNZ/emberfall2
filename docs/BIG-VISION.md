# EMBERFALL — THE BIG VISION (depth, scale, humour, professionalism)

> Van's full-vision playtest feedback, organized into a buildable plan. The throughline: make it
> BIGGER, DEEPER, FUNNIER, more PROFESSIONAL - a game that grabs people and pulls them in. This
> reframes scope upward (deliberately) and lists every enhancement, grouped + sequenced.

================================================================
## PART 1 — THE SCALE PROBLEM (the #1 theme)
================================================================
Van's repeated core note: THE GAME IS TOO SMALL. It needs to be WAY bigger with far more to
explore, especially BEFORE the catastrophe.
- TARGET: ~1 hour of play BEFORE the catastrophe/adulthood (currently a few minutes). ~5x+ the
  childhood content.
- BIGGER MAP: larger Greenhollow + much more explorable area; more regions/sub-areas; more
  density of things to find. The world should feel like a place, not a few screens.
- MORE TO DO: more quests, more NPCs, more secrets, more interactions, more variety everywhere.
- MORE TRAVEL: objectives spread out (the well far from the start, the chicken far, etc.) so
  there's a journey, not "it's right there."
- IMPLICATION: this is a bigger game than the current v1-slice scope. We lean INTO it - but still
  build region-by-region, slice-by-slice, so it stays robust. Scale via MORE proven content, not
  bigger risky builds.

================================================================
## PART 2 — STORY & THE CATASTROPHE (make it land + make it BIG)
================================================================
- MORE BUILD-UP before the catastrophe: a real childhood act (~1hr) - you bond with the village,
  do quests, explore, make the childhood choices - so losing it HURTS.
- THE CATASTROPHE NEEDS TO EXPLAIN MORE: what is happening, and why. Currently too abrupt.
  - Show/seed what's coming (foreshadowing, dread).
  - The time-skip: clarify it - were you taken away? Did you flee and return? How much time?
    People saying "you've changed" needs context (where were you, why are you back, what changed).
  - Give the player understanding of the why (tie to the Ember/oracle lie - the first hints).
- KARMA-REACTIVE NARRATIVE: if you were good/bad as a child, the narrative + how people treat the
  returning adult shifts slightly. Choices echo into the post-catastrophe world.
- MORE STORY THROUGHOUT: deeper character development, more lore, more reveals, threads that pay
  off (Pem "woz ere" -> find Pem later for a reward; the clue-hunting metagame).

================================================================
## PART 3 — HUMOUR, TONE & R18 (more Rick & Morty / Fable / silly + rude)
================================================================
Van wants the tone bible turned UP: ruder, funnier, sillier, more character, more adult (R18 but
not explicit per the existing rule). Specifics:
- More silly/rude/funny moments woven throughout (the tone bible's range, dialed up).
- DISCOVERED VIGNETTES (Fable-ish, comedic + human): walk into a house and someone's in the
  shower / dressed silly singing in the mirror / having "private alone time"; snoop a girl's
  drawer and find something embarrassing (get in trouble = a karma/comedy beat); sad, rude, funny,
  human little scenes to stumble on across the whole game.
- Social EMOTES/EXPRESSIONS (Fable-style): learn gestures/sayings - rude tongue-poke, a wink, a
  nice wave, a fart, something crude - to make villagers like/dislike you (ties to a charisma/
  reputation system). Express yourself at people.
- Funnier writing: e.g. Pip should react to the WOODEN sword ("oh, just a kid's wooden sword,
  really?") THEN give the clue about a better blade - character + comedy + a hook.
- Keep R18 within the rule: crude, rude, dark, funny - NEVER explicit sexual content, NEVER
  sexualizing minors. Adult humour, innuendo, absurdity - yes.

================================================================
## PART 4 — RPG SYSTEMS DEPTH (Fable / DnD-like)
================================================================
A much deeper RPG layer than the current light one:
- XP + LEVELS: gain XP, level up.
- STATS (DnD-style): Strength, Speed/Dexterity, Charisma, Intelligence, Wisdom, Constitution -
  affect combat, dialogue options, prices, etc.
- SKILL TREE / abilities: unlock + improve skills (combat moves, magic, social, utility).
- JOBS (Fable-style): take on jobs to earn money + skill multipliers (woodcutting, smithing,
  fishing, bounties, etc.) - a money + progression loop outside the main quest.
- COMBAT MOVES: DODGE, ROLL, BLOCK (learnable later), beyond basic attack. A real combat kit.
- CHARACTER SHEET: see your stats, attack/defense/magic-power totals, level, XP.

================================================================
## PART 5 — EQUIPMENT, ITEMS & DURABILITY (deeper gear)
================================================================
- GEAR SHOWS STATS: weapons/armour display attack, speed, defense, magic, any skills/effects;
  the character sheet shows TOTAL attack/def/magic-power from equipped gear + stats.
- DURABILITY / RESOURCES: the wooden sword wears out + breaks eventually; some items have limited
  durability, or need recharging/fueling (the LANTERN runs out -> find OIL). More resource
  mechanics = more depth + economy sinks.
- REALISTIC EQUIP/USE: equipped items show on the character + are HELD/USED visibly (hold the
  lantern out in hand; the sword swings FROM THE HAND, not the body/feet - real-looking).
- FULL ARSENAL (data-driven, per existing equipment system): stick -> wooden -> iron -> steel ->
  special swords, bow, magic, shield, armour tiers - each with stats, shown + equippable.

================================================================
## PART 6 — ECONOMY & PROGRESSION (Fable-style wealth)
================================================================
- SHOPS: real buy/sell shops (currently missing). Fair pricing (per SYSTEMS-DESIGN economy).
- MONEY LOOP: jobs + selling + finding + quests; sinks = gear, consumables, durability/fuel,
  services, eventually PROPERTY.
- BUY HOUSES / PROPERTY (Fable-style, later): own property, bigger purse upgrades, wealth as a
  progression axis + status (NPCs react to wealth).
- BALANCE: can grind a bit but not trivially out-earn costs (per SYSTEMS-DESIGN Part 1).

================================================================
## PART 7 — PROFESSIONAL POLISH (the "feel professional" gap)
================================================================
Van: "it still doesn't quite feel professional yet." The gaps:
- TEXT/FONT: more legible font; text must FIT its box (currently some long NPC text is CUT OFF) -
  auto-fit / paginate / size so nothing clips. Balanced, readable, no overflow.
- NO MESSY OVERLAPS: text-over-text, labels-over-HUD, sprites clipping - audit + fix everywhere.
  Everything should look deliberate + clean (the polish principles, enforced hard).
- HUD / MAP QUALITY: better-quality HUD + minimap + full map (crisper art, clearer, professional).
- SOUND: better SFX + music (open to BUYING quality assets once the game's further along).
- GRAPHICS: bigger/better asset library; less generic/repeated tiles + buildings (the marsh huts
  are "average/generic"); open to BUYING quality art packs later. A HUGE asset library is wanted.
- CHARACTER VARIETY: more unique NPC looks - Van already sees "double-ups and repeats" early. Need
  a much bigger, more varied cast wardrobe (LPC supports this - generate far more variants).
- VOICES?: Van asks - can characters have VOICES, not just text? (See Part 9 - feasibility.)
- CONTINUITY: areas should connect believably (the Ashen Marsh "looks like a hole"; the way in
  should run THROUGH woods against the treeline, not float on its own - better geographic
  continuity / honest transitions).

================================================================
## PART 8 — SPECIFIC FIXES & TWEAKS (from this playtest)
================================================================
- QUEST TRACKER: show ALL active quests on screen (not just 1), with toggle by MAIN / SIDE / AREA
  / individual pick, and SHORT (quest name only) vs FULL (full text) modes. (Extends the existing
  toggle/minimise.)
- The GATE across the path: looks impassable but you walk through. Decide - if it's a gate, it
  should OPEN as you approach (animate) or look passable; if blocked, block it with a reason.
  Make state match appearance.
- Spread objectives out (well farther, chicken farther) - more travel (Part 1).
- Quest-giver variety: another kid dares you to KICK the chicken; someone tells you to throw the
  hammer DOWN the well (still retrievable later) - funnier, more characterful quest framing.
- "Find a twig" -> instead BASH DOWN a tree / branch for it (not a floating pickup) - interactive.
- Pip's wooden-sword gag (Part 3).
- BLACK/EMPTY BUILDINGS: some houses are still black/nothing inside + you can't get out - fix
  interiors + always provide an exit (no trap rooms). 
- SEARCHABLE WORLD: search closets/drawers/houses; break/open more things; lots of searching +
  exploring rewarded (ties to the vignettes in Part 3).
- BOMBS: how to use them needs to be clear (tutorialise the bomb ability).
- LANTERN: held in hand + visible; runs out -> needs oil (Part 5).
- BOSSES: bigger, more moves, more challenging, each needs a TRICK to beat (not just "hit it") -
  + the player needs dodge/roll/block (Part 4) to fight well.
- DUNGEON LAYOUT: the lock/door layout is awkward - cleaner dungeon design.
- TUTORIALISE new mechanics (bombs, lantern, dodge, etc.) diegetically.

================================================================
## PART 9 — VOICES (feasibility - honest)
================================================================
Van asks for character voices. Honest options:
- FULL VOICE ACTING: not feasible for a solo/AI build (cost, recording, thousands of lines).
- AI-GENERATED VOICES: possible later - TTS per character (distinct voices) for key lines, or
  AI voice gen. Adds a lot of production weight + cost + storage. A LATER, optional, post-core
  enhancement - not now.
- CLASSIC MIDDLE GROUND (recommended): characterful "voice blips" (Animal Crossing / Undertale
  style) - a short per-character sound on text, pitched/toned per character. Cheap, charming,
  reads as "voice" without full VO. Do THIS as the realistic "voices" answer; full/AI VO is a
  far-future maybe.

================================================================
## PART 10 — HOW WE BUILD ALL THIS (the realistic plan)
================================================================
This is now clearly a BIGGER game. The discipline that makes big achievable:
1. SYSTEMS FIRST, then CONTENT SCALES: build the deep systems (XP/levels/stats/skills, jobs,
   shops/economy, durability/fuel, dodge/roll/block, emotes, searchable world, vignette framework,
   voice-blips) ONCE, robustly. Then pour CONTENT (quests, NPCs, areas, vignettes, items) as data.
2. PRIORITISE BY IMPACT: do the things that most make it feel bigger/deeper/professional first.
3. KEEP THE SLICE DISCIPLINE: one robust slice at a time, verify live, preserve, push. Big game,
   small safe steps.
4. ART/AUDIO: keep building with CC0/placeholder now; do a PAID-ASSET upgrade pass later (Van's
   open to buying) once the design + feel is locked - so money buys polish on a proven game, not a
   prototype.
5. SCOPE HONESTLY: this is a long build. The milestone structure (a complete short v1, then grow)
   still applies - but v1 itself gets bigger (a full ~1hr childhood + region + the deeper systems).

### SUGGESTED PRIORITY ORDER (impact-first)
PHASE A - PROFESSIONAL FEEL (make what exists feel pro): text auto-fit/legible font, no overlaps,
better HUD/map quality, the gate fix, black-building/interior fixes, swing-from-hand, dialogue
that fits, quest-tracker (all quests + toggle/short-full), character variety (more NPC variants),
voice-blips. [Makes the current game feel professional - high impact, mostly polish.]
PHASE B - DEPTH SYSTEMS: XP/levels/stats/skill-tree, dodge/roll/block, jobs, shops/economy,
durability+fuel (sword wear, lantern oil), gear stats + character sheet, emotes/social. [The Fable/
DnD depth.]
PHASE C - SCALE THE WORLD: 5x the childhood (~1hr), bigger Greenhollow + more areas, more quests
(spread out + funnier framing), searchable houses + breakables, comedic/rude/sad vignettes, the
catastrophe rewrite (build-up + explanation + karma-reactive), clue-hunt threads (Pem etc.).
PHASE D - CONTENT + REGIONS: the 5 regions (bigger), bosses with tricks + dodge/block, the story
deepened throughout, endings.
PHASE E - PAID POLISH: buy quality art/audio, (maybe) AI voices, final professional pass.
(These overlap; A first because it makes everything feel better immediately.)

## THE ONE LINE
Van wants Emberfall to be a BIG, deep, funny, professional adventure - Fable's freedom + systems,
Zelda's exploration, Undertale/Rick-&-Morty's heart-and-humour. We get there by building the deep
SYSTEMS once, pouring in lots of CONTENT, making it feel PROFESSIONAL (Phase A first), and
upgrading to PAID polish on a proven game. Big game, small safe steps.
