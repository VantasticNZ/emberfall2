## 1. THE FIRST FLAME (what the world believes vs what is true)

**The believed story (taught by the Oracles, told to children):** Long ago the land was cold and dark.
The **First Flame** — the Ember — came up out of the deep and gave the world its warmth. The Oracles
"shared" it: from the First Flame they lit the **kept flames**, one tended hearth-fire for every
settlement, so no village would ever be cold again. They built the chapels to bless the flames, and the
land took the Ember's name: **Emberfall**. This is the warm, true-feeling story the village lives inside.
It is what M1–M4 let the player believe — almost to the end.

**The truth (seeded in the slice, paid off region by region):** the First Flame is **alive, and it
suffers**. It did not "share" its warmth — the Oracles **broke** it: struck it into shards and **bound**
the shards, draining warmth from a screaming, captive god. The kept flames are tended fragments of that
binding. The chapels are not just blessing-houses; they are **wards** that keep the truth (and the pain)
asleep. The slice plants this as **dread under the warmth**, never states it outright. (Full reveal: the
Marsh's first lie, `STORY-AND-QUESTS.md` M8–M10.)

## 2. THE EMBERFALL (the event the land is named for)

The **Emberfall** is the founding catastrophe-dressed-as-a-blessing: the night the Oracles brought the
First Flame down ("fell" it) and bound it. Generations ago. The land calls it a gift and dates its
calendar from it ("the year of the Falling"). Children are taught it as the world's birthday; the truth is
it is the world's original wound. **It is distinct from the village's own catastrophe** (`STORY-AND-QUESTS.md`
M6, "The Night It Burned" — when Greenhollow's kept flame erupts) — that later night is a small, personal
echo of the great Emberfall. The slice does not reach M6; it lays the groundwork.

## 3. KEPT FLAMES (the warm everyday fire-fact the slice runs on)

Every Emberfall settlement tends a **kept flame** — a hearth-ember descended from the First Flame, never
allowed to die. Concrete facts the quests use:
- A kept flame is **shared, not owned**: a cold hearth is re-lit from a neighbour's kept flame or the
  chapel's. (Hence M1 "Cold Hearth" — a hearth gone out is a small village crisis with a moral edge.)
- A kept flame is **fed** (good fuel keeps it steady; bad fuel — wet wood, poor coal — makes it gutter).
- A kept flame **reads the room** (see §5): it steadies for the warm-hearted and flinches for the cruel —
  villagers read omens in it without knowing why.
- The **Hearthflame Festival** (annual) honours the kept flame; the Oracle blesses it. (Slice = pre-festival
  warmth; the festival itself is M5, post-slice.)

## 4. CHAPEL WARDS + THE DEAD EMBER-SHRINE UNDER GREENHOLLOW

**Chapel wards.** Each Oracle chapel holds a **ward** — a binding-glyph that keeps the local flame docile
and the old truth buried. In Greenhollow the ward is the **chapel's blessing-stone** (the altar). It hums
faintly; the acolyte tends it; it is "just tradition" to everyone but the Oracles. The ward's real job:
**keep the thing beneath the village asleep.**

**The dead ember-shrine under Greenhollow.** Beneath the village — reached through the **boarded cave** at
the meadow edge — lies an **old ember-shrine**: one of the First Flame's original **binding-seats**, now
**cold and dead** (dormant, not destroyed). Its centrepiece is the **weeping-flame carving** (a flame with
tears running down the rock — already canon: deed `cave_lore`). It is the slice's **lore payoff** (M4): the
first physical proof that the warm story is incomplete — a fire-shrine that is *grieving*, sealed away and
forgotten, with the chapel above keeping it quiet. **It does not "wake" in the slice** (that's deeper game);
M4's reveal is the *discovery* + the choice of what to tell the village.

## 5. FIRE RESPONDS TO PURITY (the diegetic karma hook)

The single most important slice mechanic-of-lore: **fire reads the heart.** The Ember's nature leaks into
every kept flame, so flames respond to the player's **PURITY** axis (Corrupt ↔ Pure):
- **Pure** → flames burn **steady, warm, gold**; the chapel ward hums clean; the dead shrine's carving
  glows faint when you near it.
- **Corrupt** → flames **flinch, gutter, flare wrong-coloured** (the M5 "the Flame flinched" beat writ
  small); the ward's hum sours; the shrine stays cold and the carving "weeps" darker.
- **Read, never stated:** NPCs notice "the fire likes you" / "the fire's uneasy when you're about" — they
  don't know why. This is **show, don't tell** karma feedback, and it foreshadows that fire is *alive*.
- **Hook for the build:** purity-tiered flame visuals (tint/flicker) at the chapel ward + the dead shrine +
  village hearths; an optional one-line NPC bark by purity tier. (Slice scope: at least the **chapel ward +
  the M4 shrine** respond; village hearths are a stretch goal.)

## 6. NAMING + TONE RULES (the register the slice must hold)

- **Warm village life OVER slow dread.** The slice is a *living, warm village first* — the dread is a
  distant undertone (a flame that flinches once, a cold shrine), NOT pervasive gloom. If a scene reads as
  Dark-Souls-bleak, it's wrong; punch it back toward *home*. (Dread escalates post-slice; here it's a seed.)
- **Humour lives in the PEOPLE; weight lives in the WORLD.** Characters are funny, warm, eccentric (Bryn's
  crude tavern wit, McCracken's panic, the kids' dares). The *lore/fire* is where the weight sits (the
  weeping shrine, the ward, the suffering under the warmth). Don't make the people grim or the lore jokey.
- **Light NZ flavour.** A gentle Kiwi colour in idiom + texture — "good as gold," "chur," "she'll be
  right," "the wop-wops," sheep/hens/orchards, understated friendliness, tall-poppy ribbing — **used
  sparingly as seasoning**, never a costume. Place-feel: green, damp, rural, neighbourly. (Per `TONE-AND-FEEL.md`
  this sits inside the Discworld+Undertale+Zelda register; NZ is the *accent*, not the genre.)
- **Names** (slice canon, reconciled with `WORLD-BIBLE.md`): **Mara** (baker, mother-figure), **Hodge**
  (smith), **Bryn** (tavernkeep), **Old Edda** (herbalist/wise-woman), **McCracken** (merchant), **Bram**
  (lost guardian — childhood-arc), the **chapel acolyte**, kids **Pem/Tam/Nessa**, **Sela** (Oracle). NEW for
  M2: **Maren** — an ailing elder (see SPEC open decision: new NPC vs map onto an existing one). Hard line
  (`TONE-AND-FEEL`): R18 humour OK; **never explicit; never sexualises minors.**
